Celestial Catalog

Navigate the infinite beauty of stars, planets, and galaxies beyond

Planets & Moons

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. Its famous Great Red Spot is an anticyclonic storm that has raged for over 300 years.

Saturn

Saturn is renowned for its spectacular ring system composed of billions of particles of ice and rock. It's the least dense planet in our solar system, light enough to float on water.

Mars

Known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface, Mars is the primary target of space exploration. It hosts the largest volcano in the solar system: Olympus Mons.

Stars & Constellations

Betelgeuse

One of the brightest stars in the night sky, Betelgeuse is a red supergiant nearing the end of its life. It could explode as a supernova at any time within the next 100,000 years.

Sirius

Sirius is the brightest star in the night sky as seen from Earth. It's a binary system consisting of a main-sequence white star (Sirius A) and a white dwarf (Sirius B), nicknamed "the Pup".

Polaris

The North Star has guided navigators for millennia. Located almost exactly in the extension of Earth's rotational axis, it appears motionless in the night sky.

Galaxies

Milky Way

Our home galaxy is a barred spiral containing our solar system. It features a supermassive black hole at its center (Sagittarius A*) and is part of the Local Group of galaxies.

Andromeda M31

The nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way and visible to the naked eye. It's approaching us and will merge with our galaxy to form a giant elliptical galaxy.

Messier 87

Famous for hosting the fliest directly imaged supermassive black hole in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope. It features a relativistic jet extending 5,000 light-years.

Nebulae

Orion Nebula M42

One of the brightest and most photographed nebulae. It's a stellar nursery where new stars are actively forming. Visible to the naked eye under dark skies.

Ring Nebula M57

Remnant of a dying Sun-like star. The ejected gas forms a perfect ring around a central white dwarf. It's a glimpse into our own Sun's future.

Eagle Nebula M16

Iconic site photographed by Hubble, famous for the "Pillars of Creation"-massive columns of gas and dust where new stars are being bom.

Black Holes

Sagittarius A*

The supermassive black hole at the heart of our galaxy. Recently photographed for the second time in history, after M87*. 2020 Nobel Prize in Physics for its discovery.

M87* Powehi

First black hole ever directly photographed in history. Its Hawaiian name "Powehi" means "embellished dark source of unending creation". The image required 8 telescopes across Earth.

Cygnus X-1

First stellar black hole discovered and widely accepted. It accretes matter from its companion star, emitting powerful X-rays. Subject of a famous bet between Stephen Hawking and Kip Thome.
